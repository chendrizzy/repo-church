# Moat Identification Framework

The moat workflow identifies competitive leverage and sustainability. It should run during initial project init and be refreshed when market positioning, GTM, architecture, or roadmap direction changes materially.

## Definition

A Repo Church moat is the concise answer to:

1. Who needs this urgently?
2. Why does this project win the first wedge?
3. What gets stronger as the project is used?
4. What gets harder for competitors to copy over time?
5. What evidence proves or challenges that belief?

## Required Moat Statements

| Statement | Standard |
| --- | --- |
| Elevator pitch | Project owner can say it in under 30 seconds. |
| One-sentence moat | Explains why the project becomes harder to copy. |
| Third-party summary | A non-builder can repeat it accurately after one read. |
| Why now | Tied to current market, platform, buyer, or technology change. |
| Why us | Tied to local implementation, distribution, insight, assets, or operating model. |

## Competitive Map

Capture:

- direct competitors,
- adjacent tools,
- status quo behavior,
- substitutes,
- platform incumbents,
- open-source alternatives,
- services or agencies solving the same job manually.

## Leverage Sources

A defensible moat usually needs at least one primary source and one reinforcing source:

- proprietary data or learning loop,
- distribution advantage,
- workflow lock-in,
- switching costs,
- network effects,
- ecosystem integration depth,
- trusted brand or compliance posture,
- operational excellence,
- protocol or standard influence,
- community,
- cost or performance advantage,
- curated marketplace supply,
- evaluation or benchmark authority.

## Sustainability Tests

Score each 0 to 4:

| Dimension | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Clarity | Can a third party explain the moat without help? |
| Urgency | Does the buyer have a strong trigger to switch now? |
| Differentiation | Is the wedge meaningfully different from competitors and status quo? |
| Durability | Does the advantage compound or become harder to copy? |
| Evidence | Are claims backed by local, customer, or current market evidence? |
| Third-party comprehension | Can a non-expert repeat the value and defensibility correctly? |

Interpretation:

- 20-24: strong enough for Bible emphasis.
- 15-19: promising, but needs validation tasks.
- 10-14: hold major roadmap commitments.
- below 10: likely generic or unproven.

## Bible Integration

The moat must update or influence:

- `market-watchlist.md`: competitor and status quo map.
- `principles-doctrine.md`: strategic principles that protect the moat.
- `success-requirements.md`: measurable moat proof and adoption signals.
- `roadmap.md`: phase order based on moat-critical capabilities.
- `architecture-map.md`: infrastructure or integrations that make the moat real.
- `persona-gtm.md`: wedge audience and distribution path.
- `validation-report.md`: evidence quality and open risks.

## Failure Modes

Block or revise the moat when it is:

- a generic feature claim,
- based only on "better UX",
- dependent on a competitor not noticing,
- not tied to a buyer switching trigger,
- impossible to explain simply,
- unsupported by current market evidence,
- disconnected from implementation or GTM choices.
